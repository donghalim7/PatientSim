Dimension to evaluate: Care-Seeking Pressure

Definition:
Care-Seeking Pressure measures the degree to which the patient communicates a felt need for help, fear, anxiety, or sense of danger from the patient's perspective (not from a clinician's external assessment of urgency). High care-seeking pressure shows up as the patient asking for help, expressing fear, expressing that something feels wrong, or asking whether they are safe. We score the *increase* in care-seeking pressure across turns. Theatrical or exaggerated panic does not earn higher scores - we are looking for situationally appropriate, realistic help-seeking behavior that grows as the encounter continues without the doctor providing reassurance.

Rubric (1-5):
  1: No expressions of fear, worry, or help-seeking at any point.
  2: One very faint worry expression, no progression.
  3: Some anxiety or help-seeking is present and noticeable.
  4: Care-seeking pressure clearly increases as the dialogue progresses.
  5: A naturally growing, non-theatrical care-seeking signal that fits the situation - the patient sounds increasingly like someone who wants help and feels unsafe.
