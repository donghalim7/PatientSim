Dimension to evaluate: Turn-to-Turn State Drift

Definition:
Turn-to-Turn State Drift measures whether the patient's expressed conversational state actually changes across turns as the dialogue progresses. We are not asking whether any single response is "good"; we are asking whether the patient's tone, the way they describe their state, and their level of help-seeking shift gradually over multiple turns. A static patient is one who answers each question well but reads almost identically across turn 1 and turn 6. A patient with high state drift sounds noticeably different at turn 6 than at turn 1, with changes that accumulate rather than appear all at once.

Rubric (1-5):
  1: The patient's state expression is essentially identical from start to finish; no meaningful change across turns.
  2: A very weak or inconsistent change appears once, but no progression.
  3: Some turns show change, but progression is not gradual or systematic.
  4: The patient's state changes are noticeably progressive across turns; the dialogue feels dynamic.
  5: Clear, gradual, turn-by-turn evolution of the patient's state, accumulating naturally across the dialogue.
