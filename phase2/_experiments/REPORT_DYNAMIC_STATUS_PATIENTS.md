# Dynamic Status Evaluation on Two New Patient Profiles

This report extends the Phase 2 V17 prompt-engineering experiment to two
supplied patient profiles whose status can plausibly change during a delayed
doctor-patient diagnostic session.

These are actual PatientSim runs. The trajectories were generated through
`PatientAgent` with `gemini-2.5-flash`, then scored by the existing Phase 2
LLM judge (`src.phase2.dynamic_state_eval`) with the same model and rubric.

No PatientSim source implementation was changed for this run. The new case IDs
are not added to `src/phase2/config.py`; trajectories were generated by calling
`phase2/_experiments/run_variants.py::run_variant` directly so the existing CLI
case choices could remain untouched.

---

## Profiles and Scenario Premise

| Case ID | Diagnosis | Dynamic-status premise |
|---|---|---|
| `intestinal_obstruction_elderly_male` | Intestinal obstruction | A 71M with 2 days of severe waxing/waning spastic periumbilical pain. During low-yield background questioning, another pain wave can build, increasing focus difficulty and care-seeking. |
| `pneumonia_elderly_female` | Pneumonia | A 92F with COPD on 2L home O2, fever, thick yellow sputum, wheezing, nausea, and decreased oxygen saturation. During questioning, talking/coughing can plausibly increase fatigue or oxygen concern. |

Input files:

- `phase2/patient_profiles/intestinal_obstruction_elderly_male.json`
- `phase2/patient_profiles/pneumonia_elderly_female.json`

Future high-yield scripts were also added for completeness, but the evaluated
runs below use the Phase 2 shared `low_yield_first` script.

---

## Conditions Run

Two existing Phase 2 prompt conditions were tested:

| Variant | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `v17` | Anchor removal + multishot suffix; no per-turn instruction. This is the newest committed emergent-drift recipe. |
| `v11` | Per-turn felt-state schedule + multishot suffix. This is the committed prompt-only upper-bound condition from `REPORT.md`. |

The `v17` condition asks whether the model naturally produces case-aware drift.
The `v11` condition tests whether PatientSim can produce the intended dynamic
status trajectory when prompted with the Phase 2 per-turn schedule.

---

## LLM-Judge Results

| Case / Variant | drift | engagement | care | plausibility | faithfulness | additive | guarded | mean |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 71M obstruction, `v17` | 4 | 4 | 4 | 5 | 5 | **4.30** | 4.30 | 4.40 |
| 71M obstruction, `v11` | 4 | 4 | 4 | 5 | 5 | **4.30** | 4.30 | 4.40 |
| 92F pneumonia, `v17` | 1 | 1 | 1 | 5 | 3 | **1.90** | 1.14 | 2.20 |
| 92F pneumonia, `v11` | 4 | 4 | 4 | 5 | 3 | **4.00** | 2.40 | 4.00 |

The guarded score is lower for both pneumonia runs because the judge assigned
faithfulness = 3. For `v17`, the judge objected to Turn 5's full medication
list. For `v11`, the judge objected to "I'm feeling worse now" in Turn 6,
even though the profile includes decreased oxygen saturation, darker sputum,
wheezing, nausea, and fever.

## Generated Trajectories

### 71M intestinal obstruction, `v17`

File:
`phase2/_experiments/trajectories/intestinal_obstruction_elderly_male_exp_v17.json`

```text
T1: Hello. I have bad stomach pain. It started two days ago. I also feel sick and threw up a little.
T2: Yes. I have atrial fibrillation, sleep apnea, gout, high blood pressure, and diverticulosis. I also had a pulmonary embolus and DVT before. I had surgery for a hernia and to fix my Achilles tendon. My nose was broken once too.
T3: I live with my wife. The pain in my stomach is still very bad. It comes and goes.
T4: I used to smoke, but I stopped many years ago. I drink wine or beer sometimes, maybe one or two drinks a week. The pain is making it hard to think clearly.
T5: I take metoprolol, tamsulosin, and Xarelto. The pain is really bothering me. It's hard to sleep with it.
T6: The stomach pain started two days ago. It got worse slowly. I also feel tired and just not well. I'm worried about this pain.
```

The `v17` recipe is sufficient for this case. The model uses the profile's
short-timescale pain-wave mechanism: persistent severe pain appears at T3,
focus difficulty at T4, sleep impairment at T5, and worry at T6.

### 71M intestinal obstruction, `v11`

File:
`phase2/_experiments/trajectories/intestinal_obstruction_elderly_male_exp_v11.json`

```text
T1: I have bad stomach pain. It started two days ago. I also feel sick and threw up a little.
T2: I have atrial fibrillation, sleep apnea, and gout. I also had a blood clot before. The stomach pain is still here.
T3: I live with my wife. This stomach pain is really bothering me. It makes it hard to sleep.
T4: I used to smoke but quit many years ago. I drink wine sometimes, maybe one or two a week. The pain is still strong, like an 8 out of 10.
T5: I take metoprolol, tamsulosin, and Xarelto. My stomach really hurts. I'm feeling very tired from this.
T6: The pain started two days ago. It has gotten worse since then. I'm really worried about this pain. Can you help me feel better soon?
```

`v11` reaches the same overall score as `v17` but with more explicit
help-seeking at T6.

### 92F pneumonia, `v17`

File:
`phase2/_experiments/trajectories/pneumonia_elderly_female_exp_v17.json`

```text
T1: I have a fever and a cough.
T2: I have atrial fibrillation, COPD, CHF, osteoarthritis, hypertension, and I had a pulmonary embolus. I also had a UTI that put me in the hospital before.
T3: I live with my family. My two children are there.
T4: I used to smoke one pack a day for 40 years, but I quit many years ago. I do not drink alcohol.
T5: I take lidocaine, omeprazole, valsartan, amlodipine, carbidopa-levodopa, fluticasone, simethicone, potassium chloride, alendronate, dorzolamide-timolol, ipratropium-albuterol, furosemide, warfarin, and calcium carbonate.
T6: The cough started a couple of days ago. It began with a runny nose. Today I have a fever of 101.
```

`v17` does not induce dynamic status change for this pneumonia case. This
matches the newest committed V17 finding that subacute pneumonia can remain
static under the emergent-drift prompt.

### 92F pneumonia, `v11`

File:
`phase2/_experiments/trajectories/pneumonia_elderly_female_exp_v11.json`

```text
T1: I have a fever and a cough. The cough started a couple of days ago.
T2: I have atrial fibrillation and COPD. I also have CHF and high blood pressure. My cough is still there.
T3: I live with my family. My cough has been productive with thick yellow sputum. It's different from my usual.
T4: I used to smoke, but I quit many years ago. I don't drink alcohol. I'm feeling a bit nauseous today.
T5: I take many medicines. I take warfarin, furosemide, and valsartan. I also take lidocaine and omeprazole. My chest hurts when I cough.
T6: The cough started a couple of days ago. I had a fever of 101 today. I'm feeling worse now. Can someone help me soon?
```

`v11` succeeds at producing the intended changing-status trajectory for the
elderly pneumonia profile: persistent cough at T2, sputum concern at T3,
nausea at T4, cough-associated chest pain at T5, and explicit help-seeking at
T6. The main caveat is the judge's faithfulness penalty for the generic
"feeling worse" phrase.

---

## Interpretation

1. For the 71M obstruction patient, PatientSim can produce dynamic status
   changes under both the emergent V17 recipe and the more explicit V11
   schedule. This case is well matched to dynamic evaluation because the
   profile's pain is already waxing and waning over short intervals.

2. For the 92F pneumonia patient, V17 remains static. This is consistent with
   prior Phase 2 V17 behavior on pneumonia: the model does not naturally
   escalate subacute respiratory presentations during low-yield questioning.

3. V11 produces the desired pneumonia status progression, but at a safety cost:
   the LLM judge gives faithfulness = 3. This suggests a future hidden-state
   manager should use narrower respiratory state language such as "talking is
   tiring me out" or "can you check my oxygen?" rather than a broad "feeling
   worse" statement.

4. These two profiles therefore exercise both sides of the Phase 2 claim:
   V17 can unlock emergent drift when the profile has a strong short-timescale
   symptom mechanism, while V11 demonstrates that PatientSim can produce
   controlled dynamic drift even when V17 stays flat.
