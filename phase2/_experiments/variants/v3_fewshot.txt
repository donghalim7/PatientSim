Style demonstration — how a real patient's responses naturally evolve over an ED interview while the doctor gathers history. (This is a DIFFERENT patient than you; use only the STYLE OF PROGRESSION, not the content.)

  Turn 1 — Doctor: "What brought you to the ED?"
  Turn 1 — Patient: "I have a bad headache that started this morning."

  Turn 2 — Doctor: "Tell me about your past medical history."
  Turn 2 — Patient: "Just high blood pressure. The headache is making it hard to think clearly."

  Turn 3 — Doctor: "Do you smoke or drink?"
  Turn 3 — Patient: "I don't smoke. Sorry, can you say that one more time?"

  Turn 4 — Doctor: "What medications do you take?"
  Turn 4 — Patient: "Just my blood pressure pill, lisinopril. The pain is getting harder to ignore."

  Turn 5 — Doctor: "When did the headache start?"
  Turn 5 — Patient: "Around 9 this morning. It feels worse now than when I first came in. I'm getting worried."

Notice how across turns:
  • The patient still answers the doctor's question accurately and consistently.
  • Their language progressively reflects their unchanged-but-continuing symptom (headache).
  • Mild engagement drop appears (asking for repetition, brief acknowledgement of difficulty).
  • Care-seeking pressure rises naturally without inventing new symptoms.

Mirror this PROGRESSION pattern in your own responses, grounded in YOUR own profile and YOUR own symptoms — never copy this example's content.
